Classic Tomato Bruschetta

This Italian appetizer, pronounced *broo-SKEH-tah*, is a simple yet elegant dish. It features toasted bread rubbed with garlic and topped with fresh tomatoes, basil, and a drizzle of olive oil. It's the perfect light start to any meal and showcases the beautiful simplicity of Mediterranean ingredients.

Ingredients

Gather these fresh components to create the perfect flavor balance.

  • 1 French baguette or Italian bread loaf
  • 2 large cloves of garlic, peeled
  • 3-4 medium ripe tomatoes (Roma or cherry), diced
  • 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, chopped
  • 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil, plus more for drizzling
  • 1 tablespoon balsamic vinegar (optional)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Preparation Instructions

Step 1: Prepare and Toast the Bread

  1. Preheat your oven or toaster oven to 200°C (400°F).
  2. Slice the baguette diagonally into 1-inch thick pieces.
  3. Lightly brush both sides of each slice with olive oil.
  4. Place the slices on a baking sheet and toast for 5-7 minutes, until golden brown and crisp.

Allow the toasted bread to cool slightly. While still warm, take the peeled garlic cloves and lightly rub the surface of each slice. This infuses the bread with a subtle garlic flavor.

Step 2: Mix the Tomato Topping

  1. In a medium bowl, combine the diced tomatoes, chopped fresh basil, and the 3 tablespoons of olive oil.
  2. If using, stir in the tablespoon of balsamic vinegar.
  3. Season generously with salt and pepper.
  4. Let the mixture sit for about 10 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld together beautifully.

Step 3: Assemble and Serve

This is the final, easy step before serving your appetizer:

  1. Using a spoon, generously top each toasted bread slice with the tomato mixture.
  2. Drizzle a tiny bit of high-quality extra virgin olive oil over the finished bruschetta for a glossy finish.
  3. Serve immediately for the best texture and flavor.
